Kriegsmesser

The kriegsmesser is a type of large, single-edged sword that was used in Central Europe during the late Middle Ages and Renaissance, particularly in the 14th to 16th centuries. The word "kriegsmesser" translates to "war knife" in German, highlighting its design similarities to knives rather than traditional swords.

Jian

The jian is a type of Chinese double-edged straight sword that has been used in China for over 2,500 years. Known as the "Gentleman of Weapons," the jian is characterized by its long, straight blade that typically ranges from 18 to 31 inches in length.

Broadsword

The broadsword is a term commonly used to describe a variety of wide-bladed, double-edged swords that were popular in Europe from the late medieval period through the Renaissance. Characterized by its broad, flat blade, which is typically designed for powerful cutting and slashing.

Gunto

The gunto is a type of Japanese sword that was primarily used by the Imperial Japanese Army and Navy during the late 19th and early 20th centuries, particularly throughout World War II. The design of the gunto is a blend of traditional Japanese sword-making techniques and modern military needs.
